About

Workshop Overview

Medical 3D vision lies at the intersection of artificial intelligence, computer vision, computer graphics, and medical imaging. It is becoming increasingly crucial for diagnosis, surgical planning, simulation, and personalised healthcare.

Recent advances in deep learning, generative models, neural rendering, and vision-language approaches have enabled significant progress in 3D reconstruction and generation, novel view synthesis, and immersive visualisation of medical data — including XR-supported applications and neuroscience approaches that reconstruct 3D representations from neural data.

This workshop aims to highlight innovative, AI-driven techniques for 3D vision in the medical field and to encourage discussion about the unique challenges in this domain, including data scarcity, noise, anatomical variability, robustness, interpretability, and clinical reliability — while building a lasting community around this emerging field.

CFP

Call for Papers

We invite original, previously unpublished work addressing, but not limited to, the following topics.

3D Reconstruction & Neural Rendering

AI-driven methods for medical 3D reconstruction, NeRF and Gaussian Splatting approaches, and neural rendering techniques applied to clinical imaging data.

Generative AI for Medical 3D

Diffusion models, GANs, and other generative architectures for synthesising, augmenting, or simulating medical and neural 3D data.

Vision-Language Models

VLMs and multi-modal foundation models for 3D understanding and grounding of medical images, enabling richer cross-modal representations.

XR Visualisation & Simulation

Extended reality (AR/VR/MR) tools for immersive medical visualisation, surgical training, simulation, and patient-specific planning.

Foundation & Self-Supervised Models

Large-scale pre-training and self-supervised learning strategies adapted for medical 3D vision with limited or no labelled data.

Sparse, Noisy & Heterogeneous Data

Methods that address the inherent clinical challenges of data scarcity, imaging noise, anatomical variability, and multi-modal heterogeneity.

Submission Details

Accepted papers will be published in the ECCV 2026 Workshop Proceedings. Each submission is reviewed by at least three independent reviewers following a standard double-blind review protocol.

Papers should follow the ECCV 2026 author guidelines. The submission deadline will be announced after the main conference notification (June 17, 2026). Camera-ready deadline will coincide with the main ECCV deadline.
